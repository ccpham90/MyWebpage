About Me

Lockheed Martin Systems Integration and Test engineer with two years of experience in infrared detector. I earned my Ph.D. in Chemistry from the University of California, Davis and bachelor’s in chemistry from California State University, Long Beach. I am also a former pos-doctoral scholar at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory (LBNL) in the Molecular Biophysics and Integrated Bioimaging Division. My academic career has allowed me to conduct research at various Department of Energy (DOE) national laboratories and international energy laboratories such as LBNL, Argonne National Laboratory (ANL), Stanford Linear Accelerator Center (SLAC), and Japan Synchrotron Radiation Research Institute (JASRI). Some areas of research where I have published are Nuclear Resonance Vibrational Spectroscopy (NRVS), X-ray Free Electron Lasers in X-ray Spectroscopy and Crystallography, Mössbauer, and stopped flow Fourier Transform Infrared (FTIR) spectroscopy focused on metalloenzymes relevant to alternative energy catalysis. I have publications in journals such as Journal of the American Chemical Society, Science Advances, Proceedings of National Academy of Sciences, Angewandte Chemie International Edition, and Inorganic Chemistry. My current work at Lockheed is focused on Electro-Optical Targeting Systems (EOTS) and Advance Target Waring Systems (ATWS). My diverse technical background in chemistry, biophysics, infrared detector, national laboratories, and defense has equipped me with the skills for a wide range of expertise. I am continually focused on improving and adding to my own skill set as well as others. Mentoring has been a sub-focus for me since graduate school and continues to be wherever I work. Lockheed Martin has been a great company that has afforded me many opportunities to advance my career as well as help others develop their skills.
